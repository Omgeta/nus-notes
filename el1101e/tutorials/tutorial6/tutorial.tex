      \item Structural Ambiguity only; there are multiple possible syntactical structures, and all words are unambiguous

  \item 
    \begin{enumerate}
      \item The country Turkey blames Kurdish rebels\\ 
        The bird Turkey touches Kurdish rebels 

      \item Lexical Ambiguity; "Turkey" can refer to both the country and the bird, "fingers" can refer to both blaming and touching 

      \item Turkey: bird name derived from the country ("turkey-cock") so they are polysemes\\ 
        finger: usage as blaming derived from the usage of touching, so they are polysemes
    \end{enumerate}

  \item

      \item Entailment: if the govt. will redouble its efforts, it will make efforts 
      \item Presupposition: if the govt. will redouble its efforts, it suggests previous efforts have been made
      \item Neither: just a rephrasing, they entail each other
    \end{enumerate}

      \item Presupposition: if I finished my math homework, then I must have been assigned homework in the first place 
      \item Neither: finishing my math homework does not imply I finished all my homework
    \end{enumerate}
